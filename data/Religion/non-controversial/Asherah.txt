Hellenistic, and Roman periods in Egypt there was a strong tendency towards
syncretism of goddesses and Athirat/Ashrtum then seems to have disappeared, at
least as a prominent goddess under a recognizable name. In Israel and Judah.
Some speculate that between the 10th century BC and the beginning of their

universal among Jews. The majority of biblical scholars accept that Asherah at
one time was worshiped as the consort of Yahweh, the national god of Israel.
